Emerging Themes
Supplier Sourcing as an Alternative to Price Increases
New
›
Rather than accepting a price increase recommendation, P01 repeatedly asked whether the agent could surface alternative suppliers who sell the same ingredient at the original price. This came up twice unprompted and was named as the single "magic wand" change.
Why it matters
This reframes the agent's cost-spike response from "raise your price" to "here is another way to protect your margin" — a meaningfully different and potentially stickier value proposition that the current prototype does not address.
"I would do the thing that I was talking about, where I can reference product lists from all the suppliers around me, and instead of raising the price of this dish, it would give me somewhere else to buy the cheese that we're, we're losing our on here."
Recipe/Ingredient Data Staleness as a Trust Blocker
New
›
P01 flagged that the cost data currently in Toast may be outdated because dishes evolved after the recipe was originally entered, making AI recommendations feel less reliable without a way to verify the underlying data.
Why it matters
Data staleness is a pre-condition trust blocker: even an operator with high brand trust in Toast will hesitate to act on recommendations if they suspect the underlying recipe/cost data is wrong. The agent may need a "verify your data" prompt before surfacing pricing recommendations.
"I would like them to maybe show me the menu, like, the menu item, including the recipe, and see if it's correct. Because I have lots of stuff in my cost right now. That was put in a long time ago, and then things evolved into a new dish, and I would like to see what we're working with right now."
Staff Notification as a Required Post-Publish Action
New
›
P01 volunteered — without being prompted — that approve-and-publish should automatically trigger an internal note reminding staff that the menu has changed.
Why it matters
This aligns with the "Downstream Action Deep-Links" theme from P04, but P01 frames it as an automated internal notification rather than a deep-link checklist — suggesting operators may want different delivery mechanisms for the same downstream step.
"I think I would want it to automatically change the menu, but also to leave a note that there's a menu change, and everybody should be careful to look at it."
Iterative Chat Creates Kitchen Ops Complexity
New
›
When shown the ability to interactively adjust the recommendation via chat, P01 flagged that every pricing iteration would require printing new kitchen recipes, making repeated chat-based adjustments impractical.
Why it matters
The agentic "chat to refine" interaction model assumes a digital-only workflow, but kitchen operations are still paper-based in many restaurants. This creates a hidden cost to iterative approval flows that the prototype does not account for.
"This would be helpful, but I would need to keep printing out new recipes to get to the kitchen, and it would, over time, just be very complicated to keep changing it like that."
Video/Tutorial Onboarding as a Trust Bridge
New
›
P01 asked for email notifications that link to YouTube tutorials showing how the feature works, and for an example of the agent's output before committing credits.
Why it matters
For operators who haven't yet used ToastIQ, a "show me it working" proof point is the bridge between awareness and first action — not just UI copy. This has onboarding and marketing channel implications (email + video, not just in-product tooltips).
"I think that emails will be great, and maybe even emails that link to YouTube videos of somebody using this new stuff."

Standout Insight
Key Takeaway
P01's "magic wand" request is not a better UI — it's a different product. Instead of recommending a price increase, the agent should recommend an alternative supplier who sells the same ingredient at the original cost. This reframes the entire cost-spike use case from "raise prices" to "protect margins."
