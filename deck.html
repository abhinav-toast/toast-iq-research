02 — Key Learnings
5 Cross-Cutting Themes
Surfaced across all 4 sessions — ranked by frequency and business impact
💳
Credits / monetization friction — universal signal
Every operator noticed the credits model unprompted. Common ask: show dollar value per credit and projected ROI before the confirm gate. P04 — the highest-intent user — would abandon the workflow rather than spend.
4 / 4 participants
🔐
Owner sign-off gate — structural requirement, not preference
P04: organizational hierarchy. P02: process governance. P03: prior trauma with Uber Eats auto-publishing. Three different reasons, same behavior. The review step will remain even as trust grows — operators want the agent to reduce cognitive load, not remove them from the loop.
3 / 4 participants
🏪
Multi-location publish scope — deal-breaker for chains
P02 and P04 both operate 4 locations with shared menus. Without a scope selector and Menu Masters integration, they'd have to repeat the workflow per store. An agent that solves 80% of the workflow but leaves 20% as harder-than-before manual work is net-negative.
2 / 4 participants
📊
Data provenance — prerequisite for trust, not a nice-to-have
P03 asked "where does this data come from?" multiple times. Operators won't act on numbers they can't trace. Unanchored projections (e.g., "$847 monthly recovery") erode trust — every estimate needs an auditable basis operators can independently verify.
2 / 4 participants
📣
Post-publish coordination — cognitive overhead that follows every price change
Approving a change is not the last step. Staff notifications, website updates, social posts, kitchen reprints — operators carry these mentally and manage them manually. A downstream checklist with deep-links into Toast tools addresses real cognitive overhead.
2 / 4 participants
03 — Design Feedback
What the Prototype Revealed
Specific reactions to the UI and interaction model across sessions
⛔ Credits gate at highest-intent moment Friction
The paywall appears exactly when the operator has decided to act. P04 described a complete workflow abandonment rather than spend without seeing ROI. The placement breaks the trust loop at its most critical point.
"I'd just do the research myself rather than pay for something I'm not sure about." — P04
🪄 Unanchored projections erode trust Friction
P03 found the $847 monthly recovery implausible — not because it was wrong, but because it wasn't tied to anything falsifiable. The fix is simple: show the unit sales assumption the number rests on. "If you sold 847 units last month, here's what that means."
"Where does this number come from? Show me the math." — P03
🗂️ No persistent draft / review state Gap
The current prototype presents a recommendation and asks for immediate confirmation. All three operators who require sign-off have no place to park the decision for later review or delegation. The workflow needs a durable "draft" state that survives sessions and routes to the right approver.
🔗 Modifier cascade not surfaced before confirm Gap
P04 had a live revenue incident caused by a base price change that silently broke modifier pricing. The prototype shows only the primary item change. The agent must map and display every downstream entity affected — modifier groups, add-ons, bundles — before the confirm affordance appears.
✅ Concept legibility and progressive disclosure Positive
P02 understood and articulated the agentic shift unprompted. The two-tier card format (headline visible, detail on expand) worked well — P01 and P04 appreciated the headline-first approach and noted they rarely needed the full detail to decide.
🌅 Timing and cadence mismatch Friction
P02's "effective tomorrow" reaction exposed a key issue: urgency framing is calibrated for high-volatility cost environments, not QSR quarterly pricing cycles. Recommendation timing must be derived from the operator's actual cadence, not hardcoded into the template.
"We don't change prices like that — we do it quarterly." — P02
04 — Concept Feedback
How Each Operator Received the Agent
Four distinct mental models, one shared conclusion
P01 Owner · FSR Independent
Agent as smarter notepad
Does pricing math on paper today. The agent slots directly into that workflow. High trust, low conceptual friction — blocked only by stale recipe data in Toast. Receptivity 5/5.
"I'd use it in a rush and trust it — but I'd want my staff in the loop too."
P02 Menu & Ops Manager · QSR Chain
Agent as upgrade from Q&A
Already uses Toast IQ daily. Immediately articulated the agentic shift unprompted — clearest validation in the study. Blocked operationally by Menu Masters gap and delivery tier pricing. Receptivity 4/5.
"You're asking it to do something for you instead of just asking a question."
P03 Owner · FSR Multi-Concept
Agent as co-pilot with permission gate
Shaped by Uber Eats auto-publishing his menu descriptions without approval. Didn't reject the concept — rejected the assumption of autonomy. Ideal agent proposes, never acts unilaterally. Receptivity 4/5.
"I want to see where the numbers come from before I trust them."
P04 Operator · FSR Burger/Bar
Agent as daily brief tool
Wants intelligence bundled into a single daily check-in, not always-on interruption. Hardest monetization veto — would abandon the tool over credits. Modifier cascade concern stems from a real revenue incident. Receptivity 4/5.
"Give me a morning news brief — not constant pings."
Shared Conclusion
All four operators understand and want the concept. None are waiting for it to improve. They're waiting for the execution to catch up to what they already believe is possible. The research shows no attitudinal barrier — only structural ones.
05 — Recommendations
What to Fix Before Shipping
Ranked by blocking severity — must-fix to ship vs. high-value improvements
Must 1
Replace credits gate with ROI-first reveal
Show per-credit dollar value and projected revenue recovery before the confirm gate. Consider a free first-use or bundled-usage model for the initial rollout. The current placement suppresses the highest-intent operators.
Fix: surface cost transparency before commitment → consider bundled credits in base plan
Must 2
Build persistent draft / reviewer-assignment state
The agent must support a durable draft that survives sessions and routes to a designated approver role (owner, operator, manager) — not just the initiating user. Auto-apply must not exist at any trust level at launch.
Fix: propose → draft → review → publish pipeline with role-based routing
Must 3
Surface full modifier cascade before confirm
Every pricing change must show all affected modifiers, add-ons, substitutions, and bundles with their current and post-change values before the confirm affordance appears. This is a trust signal, not a nice-to-have — P04's incident shows the real revenue risk.
Fix: cascade_mapper runs on every change skill before recommendation renders
High 4
Anchor every projection to a verifiable unit assumption
Never show a dollar or percentage estimate without the unit sales assumption it rests on. "Based on 847 units sold last month" makes the number falsifiable — operators can check the math against their own knowledge of their business.
Fix: AnchoredProjection component required for all estimates — no raw numbers
Next 5
Add publish scope selector + Menu Masters integration
Multi-location operators need to choose which locations a change applies to at the confirm step. Without Menu Masters integration, 4-location operators must repeat the workflow per store, making the agent net-negative for their segment.
Fix: scope selector at draft creation; Menu Masters API for chain menus
06 — Opportunities
Unmet Needs Worth Pursuing
Problems operators articulated unprompted — not addressed by the current prototype
🧲 Supplier alternatives — protect margin, don't just raise prices Highest signal
P01's "magic wand": when ingredient costs spike, find a cheaper supplier at the original price instead of recommending a price hike. This reframes the entire cost-spike use case from reactive pricing to proactive margin protection — a fundamentally different and potentially stickier value prop.
Source: P01 · unprompted · single-location FSR owner
🕸️ Modifier cascade awareness as a trust anchor Highest signal
P04 had a real revenue incident from a base price change that silently created modifier arbitrage. He now does this analysis manually before every change. An agent that automatically maps downstream impact would eliminate a manual step that currently carries real business risk.
Source: P04 · live incident · multi-location FSR operator
📍 Competitive benchmarking as a distinct module Strong signal
P02 asked for regional competitive pricing as a promotional strategy input. P03 described manually researching nearby restaurant prices today. Neither was prompted. Both framed automation as a genuine upgrade to a regular operational task — not a vanity feature.
Source: P02 + P03 · independently surfaced · different segments
🌅 Daily brief format as an autonomy entry point Strong signal
P04's unprompted "morning news brief" framing is the most nuanced model for agentic UX: intelligence bundled into one daily check-in that fits around existing routines. This is a low-friction path to autonomy — the agent acts overnight, the operator reviews in the morning.
Source: P04 · unprompted · aligns with Phase 4 autonomy pattern
07 — What's Next
Path Forward
From research to product — near-term and medium-term
Now — Must-fix before next test
Resolve the 3 blockers
  • Redesign credits reveal — show ROI before the gate, not after
  • Build draft/review pipeline with role-based approver routing
  • Surface modifier cascade map on every pricing recommendation
Near-term — Structural improvements
Close the workflow gaps
  • Add publish scope selector (this location / all / select)
  • Anchor all projections to unit sales assumptions
  • Calibrate urgency language per operator pricing cadence
  • Post-publish downstream checklist with deep-links
Medium-term — Product expansion
Build toward the opportunities
  • Supplier alternatives module — proactive margin protection
  • Competitive benchmarking signal or standalone module
  • Menu Masters integration for chain operators
  • Daily brief format as Phase 4 autonomy entry point
Research — Continue building evidence
Next round of testing
  • Re-test revised credits model with P04-type power users
  • Test draft/review flow with owner + manager pairs
  • Validate cascade map concept with multi-location operators
