Emotional experience
Anxious
Margin pressure is stressful. Manual math feels inadequate. P01: "I do it longhand on a notepad."
Curious + hopeful
Agent surfacing a recommendation feels like a genuine upgrade over current process. P02: "That would be nice."
Skeptical
Unanchored numbers trigger distrust. P03 became suspicious of $847 projection without a unit basis. Can't verify = can't trust.
Frustrated
No tool support for the approval process. Operators are on their own to present the case. P04: "Myself and the owners sitting down..."
Cautious
Willing to approve but not auto-apply. Credits model triggers friction right at moment of highest intent. P04: "Right off the bat, don't like it."
Relieved, but incomplete
Publishing feels good but the work isn't done. Operators carry the follow-up checklist in their heads. Agent treats this as the finish line.
Pain points
P01 · P03 Reactive, not proactive. Operators discover cost problems through manual invoice review or POS dips — no unified alert surface. P03 Quarterly batch constraint. Printed menus mean changes can only happen at review cycles — urgency framing from the agent is irrelevant.
P01 · P02 · P03 · P04 One answer, not options. Agent recommends "raise the price." Operators want a set of paths — alternative supplier, portion reduction, bundling — with tradeoffs. P02 Urgency misalignment. "Effective tomorrow" framing conflicts with quarterly-cadence operators. Erodes trust in the recommendation engine.
P03 Unanchored projections. "$847 monthly recovery" with no unit basis. Operators can't verify the math → they don't trust the number. P01 Stale data. Recipes in Toast not updated after dish evolution. Operator knows the data may be wrong but has no way to flag it before trusting recommendations. P03 Benchmark blindness. Geographic averaging doesn't account for concept tier. P03's upscale concept gets told it's "overpriced" vs. nearby fast casual.
P02 · P03 · P04 No draft state. Every operator needs to involve a second person before publishing. There's no way to save, share, or route a recommendation package within Toast. P04 Silent cascade risk. Base price changes can silently break modifier arbitrage. P04 had a live incident. Agent currently treats pricing as item-level.
P02 · P04 Multi-location repetition. Without Menu Masters integration, approving = doing it 4 times. Makes the workflow net-negative for chains. P02 · P04 Credits friction at peak intent. Gate appears exactly when operator is ready to act. P04 would abandon workflow and investigate manually rather than pay. P03 Delivery menu gap. Approved change does not cascade to DoorDash/Uber Eats pricing tiers.
P01 · P02 · P04 Invisible follow-up. Staff notification, menu reprinting, website update, social post — all happen in the operator's head. Agent treats Publish as done. P01 No before/after visibility. Post-publish, operators can't easily see what changed vs. what it was, or whether margin improved.
Design opportunities
Daily brief Morning digest card. Bundle cost spikes, performance alerts, and pending recommendations into one dismissible daily card — delivered before the day starts, not mid-service. Proactive surface Contextual menus-page trigger. Surface the recommendation in context (on the item, not in a separate tool) with a "projected update" deeplink.
Multiple paths Options, not answers. Respond to cost spikes with 3–4 paths (raise price / alternative supplier / reduce portion / bundle) with projected margin impact per option. Operator decides. Cadence-aware Operator-derived urgency. Frame urgency based on how this operator actually works — "for your next quarterly review" vs. "effective tomorrow."
Provenance Show the math. Every projection anchored to a verifiable unit assumption: "Based on 847 units in March." Operator can open POS and check. Data health Verify before recommending. Run data health check first. If stale: show a DataHealthCard ("your recipe was last updated 8 months ago") instead of a potentially wrong recommendation. Concept-aware benchmarks Tier-aware peer groups. Benchmark by concept tier, not just geography. Surface peer group composition ("28 full-service, avg check $35–55") so operators can evaluate the comparison.
Persistent draft Draft state + role routing. A named, persistent change package that routes to the right approver role (owner/GM) and waits — does not expire overnight. Cascade map Show the downstream graph. Before any recommendation, run cascade mapper. Surface affected modifiers, bundles, and delivery items upfront — before the confirm step.
Scope selector Publish once, propagate everywhere. Declare scope (this location / all / select) at draft creation. Integrate with Menu Masters for chains. Rollback Show the exit. Tell the operator whether and how the change can be reversed before they approve. Reversibility unlocks willingness to act. Credits Move the gate earlier. Translate credits to dollar value at the point of recommendation, not the confirm step. Let operators see ROI before intent peaks.
Downstream checklist Post-publish coordination package. Staff notification (drafted), website prompt, social suggestion — built into the draft as a required step, not an afterthought. Before/after summary Close the loop. Show a before/after price table with total delta. P01: "right to all the important information."

Current experience
Two critical drops: Stage 3 (trust trough — unverifiable data) and Stage 4 (process gap — no draft state). The hope peak at Stage 2 is real and fragile — it depends entirely on what comes next.
Summary
Critical pain points
No draft/review state. Approval loop happens entirely outside Toast — no persistence, no routing, no record.
Unanchored projections. Numbers without a verifiable basis feel fabricated. Stage 3 trust trough collapses early interest.
Silent cascade risk. Agent treats pricing as item-level. Modifier arbitrage is a real, documented incident (P04).
Agent treats Publish as the end. Post-publish coordination is invisible — operators carry it in their heads.
Credits gate at peak intent. Friction appears exactly when the operator is most ready to act.
Highest-leverage opportunities
Persistent draft + role routing. The biggest structural gap. No other improvement matters if approval can't happen inside Toast.
Provenance + projection basis. Attach a verifiable unit assumption to every number. Lets operators check the math themselves — the minimum bar for trust.
Options, not answers. Alternative supplier path reframes the product from "pricing tool" to "margin protection tool." Highest differentiation.
Downstream coordination package. Built into the draft as a required step — not a post-publish afterthought.
Cascade map before confirm. Surface affected modifiers/bundles before the approve step, not after publish.
What's working
Concept recognition. Every participant understood the agent model immediately. No orientation confusion — the shift from Q&A to action-taking landed positively.
Confidence scores + reasoning. P02 appreciated explicit reasoning in the UI as a trust-building signal.
Downstream checklist. Even a static reminder was appreciated by P02. P03 accepted it naturally.
Menus-page surfacing. P01 and P03 found the IQ bar in context natural and well-placed — don't move it to a separate tool.
Before/after summary. Post-publish table described by P01 as "right to all the important information."
The design constraint that runs through every stage
Operators are not trying to delegate decisions to the agent. They are trying to make better decisions faster — within their own organizational process, with their own sign-off authority, on their own cadence. Every stage where the agent assumes otherwise creates a trust breakdown. The agent is hired as a research assistant and work-product preparer. Design accordingly.
